04-charleston.png
============================================================

The Charleston is what makes American Mahjong different from every other version.

Before play begins, you pass tiles you don't want and (hopefully) get tiles you need. It goes: Right, Across, Left — then back: Left, Across, Right — then an optional courtesy pass.

It sounds complicated. It's not. MAHJ walks you through every pass, step by step, with practice drills.

Did you know American Mahjong has 8 Jokers?

Most other versions of mahjong have zero. The Jokers are what make the American version unique — they're wild cards that can substitute in any group of 3 or more identical tiles.

But here's the catch: you can NEVER use a Joker in a pair. And you can never call a discarded Joker. They're powerful but they have rules.

Learn all the Joker rules in Module 5 — link in bio

Pro tip: After the deal, keep 2-3 candidate hands in mind — not just one.

Too many beginners lock into a single hand immediately and then panic when they don't get the tiles they need.

Stay flexible. Look at your strongest suit. Count your pairs and triples. Check for patterns (all even? lots of Winds?). THEN start narrowing down after the Charleston.

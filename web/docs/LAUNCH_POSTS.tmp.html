LinkedIn

The product I wish existed when I started learning American Mahjong

Three months ago, I sat down to my first American Mahjong game. I knew the tiles, sort of. I knew the basic idea. What I didn't know was almost everything else — and within minutes, it was painfully obvious.

American Mahjong has an unusually steep learning curve. There's a yearly card from the National Mah Jongg League that lists every legal winning hand — but the notation is cryptic, the categories are unintuitive, and the rules around jokers, calling, and scoring are specific enough that generic "mahjong" tutorials actively confuse you.

Most online resources assume you already know the basics. The good ones are expensive classes. The free ones are walls of text.

So I built MAHJ — an interactive, beginner-first learning tool for American Mahjong.

What's inside:

  • 14 progressive lessons covering tiles, setup, the Charleston, calling, jokers, strategy, defense, scoring, and etiquette
  • An interactive practice game against AI opponents that pursue real hand patterns
  • A printable two-page cheat sheet designed for someone sitting at their first real game
  • A scoring calculator and a searchable glossary of 90+ terms

Technical stack: Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS 4. Pattern-matching engine built from scratch (can't reproduce NMJL card hands due to copyright, so the practice library uses 24 original patterns representing the real card's categories). Deployed on Vercel, iOS wrapper via Capacitor.

What surprised me: The hardest part wasn't the game logic — it was making sure the teaching content was accurate and accessible. Several full audits caught places where I'd accidentally drifted into generic mahjong territory. American Mahjong has rules that diverge significantly from Chinese/Japanese variants, and getting them exactly right took more care than I expected.

If you know someone who plays, or who's trying to learn, I'd love for them to try it.

👉 https://welcome2mahj.com

Feedback welcome — especially from anyone who teaches mahj or runs a club.

Quick Tips for Sharing

General:

  • Lead with a hook, not a pitch. "My first game was a disaster" > "Announcing MAHJ" — every time.
  • Be specific. "14 lessons, 3 bots, printable cheat sheet" beats "lots of features."
  • Ask for feedback. People respond better to "I need help improving this" than "check out my app."

Reddit:

  • Post on weekday afternoons (US time) for best visibility
  • Respond to every comment quickly — mods notice engagement
  • Don't crosspost verbatim — adapt for each sub

Facebook:

  • Read group rules first — some ban self-promotion
  • Replying in existing "How did you learn?" threads often works better than a standalone post
  • Tag relevant friends who you know are learning

Twitter:

  • Post the hook thread, then pin it
  • Reply to anyone asking about mahj in general
  • Use hashtags sparingly — 1-2 per post

For all platforms:

  • Don't mass-post the same text — algorithms flag it as spam
  • Respond to every reply, even "cool!" — engagement drives reach
  • If someone says "I got confused at X" — THANK them and ask follow-ups. That's gold.
  • Don't apologize for promoting your work. You made something useful.

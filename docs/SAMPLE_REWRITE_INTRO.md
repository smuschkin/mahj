## Screen 2 of 4 — What is American Mahjong?

# What is it?

American Mahjong is a **tile game** played by **4 people** around a table.

Think of it like rummy — but with beautiful tiles instead of cards, a unique trading ritual, and *wild jokers* that make every hand exciting.

**Each game takes about 15–30 minutes.** You'll usually play several in an evening with friends.

## Screen 3 of 4 — How you win

# How you win

Your goal: **be the first to build a complete "hand" of 14 tiles** that matches one of the official winning patterns.

You'll draw tiles, swap tiles, and watch what others discard — slowly working toward your target hand.

When you complete it, you call **"Mahjong!"** and you win the round.

> 💡 **Heads up:** the official winning patterns come from a small card published every year by the National Mah Jongg League. You'll need to buy one to play real games — but you don't need it yet to learn. We'll teach you everything *around* the card first.

## What changed from the source guide

| Original | Rewritten | Why |
|---|---|---|
| All overview info on one dense page | 4 short screens, one idea each | Progressive disclosure |
| "152 tiles, 4 racks, 2 dice, NMJL card, chips, pusher..." dumped upfront | Equipment introduced *only when needed* in Module 2 | No vocabulary dumps |
| "Game Length: 15-30 min, Skill Level: Intermediate, Scoring: Money..." as table | Conversational sentences inline | Plain language |
| No call-to-action | Every screen ends with a button | Active engagement |
| No expectation-setting about the card | Honest, friendly explanation: "you'll need to buy one for real games, but not yet" | Trust + sets MAHJ apart from a card-reproduction app |
| No path preview | Numbered 12-module preview with "30 minutes to your first game" promise | Motivation + commitment |
| No interactive practice | (Lesson 1 will end with tile-tap drill) | Active learning |

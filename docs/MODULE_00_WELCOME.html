🀄 What is American Mahjong?

American Mahjong is a tile game played by 4 people around a table.

Think of it like rummy — but with beautiful tiles instead of cards, a unique trading ritual, and wild jokers that make every hand exciting.

Each game takes about 15–30 minutes. You'll usually play several in an evening with friends.

Screen 3 of 3

🏆 How you win

Your goal: be the first to build a complete "hand" of 14 tiles that matches one of the official winning patterns.

You'll draw tiles, swap tiles, and watch what others discard — slowly working toward your target hand.

When you complete it, you call "Mahjong!" and you win the round.

Reality Check

📋 Before you start

A 60-second look at what you're getting into. No surprises.

  • ✓
    You'll need 3 friends to play a real game. Don't have them yet? No problem — practice on the app first, then we'll show you where to find local groups.
  • ✓
    You'll need a set, racks, and dice for live play. A starter set runs about $40–80. The official card is $14/year from the National Mah Jongg League (we'll link you when you're ready).
  • ✓
    The first few games will feel slow and confusing. That's completely normal. Mahjong has a longer learning curve than most card games — and it's worth it.
  • ✓
    Don't try to memorize the card on day one. Nobody does. You'll pick it up by playing.
  • ✓
    Plan about 2 hours for your first real session. Setup is slow at first, and you'll have lots of questions. Pace yourself.
  • ✓
    You probably won't win your first 3–5 games. That's not a failure — that's the learning curve. Trust the process.
  • ✓
    This app teaches you the game, not the card. When you're ready for real play, you'll buy the official card separately.

Meet the tiles

An American Mahjong set has 152 tiles.

That sounds like a lot — but they're organized into just 5 simple groups. Once you know the groups, you know them all.

Let's go one group at a time.

💡 You don't need to memorize anything. Just look and recognize. We'll quiz you gently at the end.

Screen 2 of 9

The 3 number suits

Most of the tiles are numbered 1 through 9, in three different "suits."

Think of suits like the colors in a deck of cards (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣) — same numbers, different look.

The three suits are: Bams, Cracks, Dots.

Meet Bams →
Screen 3 of 9

🎋 Bams (Bamboo)

Bams look like little stalks of bamboo.

There are 9 of them (numbered 1 through 9), and 4 of each number in the set — so 36 Bam tiles total.

1 Bam (Bird)
5 Bam
9 Bam
⚠️ The 1 Bam is special — it's almost always drawn as a bird instead of a bamboo stalk. Don't let it fool you.

🔵 Dots (Circles)

Dots are exactly what they sound like: circles arranged in patterns.

A 1 Dot has one circle, a 2 Dot has two, all the way up to 9 Dot.

Numbered 1 through 9, 4 of each = 36 Dot tiles.

5 Dot
9 Dot
✅ That's all 3 number suits. Bams + Cracks + Dots = 108 tiles total.

Practice Drill 1

Suit Sorter

Quick check: which suit?

Show one tile at a time. User taps Bam / Crack / Dot.

  • 5 random tiles
  • Instant feedback
  • Must get 4/5 to continue (or "Try again" with a new set)

→ "Nice — you've got the number suits down. Now for the special tiles."

🌬️ Honor tiles, Part 1: Winds

Honor tiles are special — they don't have numbers.

⭐ Jokers — the wild card

Jokers are American Mahjong's secret weapon.

There are 8 Jokers in the set, and they can stand in for any tile in a group of 3 or more identical tiles.

JOKER
JOKER
JOKER

(8 in the full set)

⚠️ Big rule: Jokers cannot be used in pairs. We'll explain why in Module 4. For now, just remember: jokers in pungs and kongs, never in pairs.

🗣️ How to say it

Real talk — beginners are nervous about saying things wrong at the table. Here's a cheat sheet so you walk in confident.

TermSay it likeNotes
Mahjong"mah-zhong"Soft "j" — like the "s" in "treasure," not a hard "j"
Bam"bam"What everyone calls Bamboo at the table
Crack"crak"Table name for "Character" tiles
Dot"dot"Easy one
Pung"puhng"Three of a kind. Rhymes with "lung," not "loon"
Kong"kahng"Four of a kind. Rhymes with "song"
Quint"kwint"Five of a kind (you'll see these later)
Joker"JOH-ker"Same as in cards
Charleston"CHAR-uls-tuhn"Like the city in South Carolina
Soap"sope"Slang for the White Dragon (looks like a blank bar of soap)
Dragon"DRA-gun"Red, Green, or White (Soap)
Wind"wind"As in weather — N, E, W, S
💡 If you're not sure how to say something at a real table, just point at the tile and ask. Every experienced player remembers being a beginner — they'll happily help.
Practice Drill 2

Speed Tap

Grid of 12 random tiles. User taps every matching tile as fast as they can.

  • 30 second timer
  • Score = correct taps − wrong taps
  • 3 rounds, each with a different category:
    1. Tap all the dragons
    2. Tap all the 3s (any suit)
    3. Tap all the honor tiles (winds + dragons)

→ "You're getting fast. One more thing before we move on."

Implementation notes

  • Tile rendering: Unicode mahjong characters work for v0; commissioned SVGs in Phase 4
  • State: per-screen progress in localStorage
  • Animations: subtle fade-in on tile reveal; haptic tap feedback on mobile
  • Accessibility: aria-labels on every tile; never rely on color alone
  • Tone: warm and encouraging — "You're getting fast!" not "Correct!"
  • Disclaimer footer: always visible

Setup & Dealing

From a pile of tiles to "Charleston, ready?"

Goal: By the end, you can confidently set up a fresh game from scratch — wash, build the wall, roll for East, take seats, break the wall, and deal.

Estimated time: 7–9 minutes

Prerequisite: Module 1 (Tile Trainer)

Unlocks: Module 3 (The Charleston)

Why this matters: Setup is where new players feel the most lost. After this module, you'll be able to set up the table — something even some intermediate players get wrong.

Lesson Screens

Screen 1 of 8

🪑 Where we're going

Before you can play a hand, four things have to happen:

  1. Wash — mix all 152 tiles face-down
  2. Build — stack the tiles into 4 walls
  3. Sit — roll dice to decide who's East
  4. Break & Deal — open the wall and pass out 13 tiles each

Sounds like a lot, but each step takes seconds once you've done it. We'll walk through them one at a time.

Step 1: Wash →
Screen 2 of 8

🌀 Step 1: Wash the tiles

Put all 152 tiles face-down on the table and gently swirl them around with your hands.

Everyone helps. The goal is just to thoroughly mix them — this is your "shuffle."

You'll hear the satisfying clack of tiles. That sound is half the reason people love mahjong.

💡 Don't flip tiles face-up while washing — keep them all face-down so nobody sees what's coming.
Step 2: Build →
Screen 3 of 8

🧱 Step 2: Build the wall

Each player builds a wall in front of themselves: 19 tiles long, stacked 2 tiles high.

4 players × 19 × 2 = 152 tiles — exactly the whole set.

The four walls form a square in the middle of the table. This is "the wall" you'll draw from all game.

Step 3: Sit →
Screen 4 of 8

🎲 Step 3: Roll for East

East deals first and is the most important seat — so you need to fairly decide who gets it.

Have someone roll two dice. The highest roll becomes East. (Re-roll any ties.)

Example roll: 4 + 3 = 7

💡 East has the best odds of winning the first hand, so it's worth a fair roll. After each round, the East seat passes to the next player.

Step 5: Break the wall →
Screen 6 of 8

🔨 Step 5: Break the wall

This is the trickiest part for beginners — but the rule is simple once you see it.

East rolls the two dice again. The total tells you which wall to break, and where to break it.

  1. Count players counter-clockwise from East, starting at 1 with East. The number you land on is the player whose wall you'll break.
  2. From the right end of that wall, count in stacks equal to the dice total. Lift those stacks aside — that's where the deal starts.

Step 6: Deal →
Screen 7 of 8

🃏 Step 6: Deal the tiles

Starting from where you broke the wall, deal tiles counter-clockwise in groups of 4, going around the table:

  • East gets 4 tiles, then South, West, North
  • Repeat: another 4 to each player
  • Repeat once more — everyone now has 12
  • Then deal 1 tile each around the table — everyone has 13
  • East gets one extra tile — East has 14 (because East starts the play)

Almost there →

✨ You're set up!

The wall is built, seats are taken, tiles are dealt. Everyone is looking at their 13 (or 14) tiles, sorting them on the rack.

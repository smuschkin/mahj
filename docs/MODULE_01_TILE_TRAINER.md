# Module 1 — Tile Trainer

**Goal:** By the end of this module, the learner can recognize all 152 tiles in an American Mahjong set and name each category confidently.

**Estimated time:** 6–8 minutes
**Prerequisite:** Module 0 (Welcome)
**Unlocks:** Module 2 (Setup & Dealing)

**Readability principles applied:** progressive disclosure (2–3 tiles at a time), define-before-use, show-then-tell, active practice within 60 seconds, confidence checkpoint at the end.

---

## Structure

```
Lesson screens:    9
Practice drills:   2 (Flashcards + Speed Tap)
Confidence check:  5 questions
Total screens:     ~17
```

## Screen 2 of 9 — The 3 number suits

# The 3 number suits

Most of the tiles are **numbered 1 through 9**, in three different "suits."

Think of suits like the colors in a deck of cards (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣) — same numbers, different look.

The three suits are: **Bams, Cracks, Dots.**

[ Meet Bams → ]

---

## Screen 3 of 9 — Bams 🎋

# Bams (Bamboo)

**Bams** look like little stalks of bamboo.

There are **9 of them** (numbered 1 through 9), and **4 of each number** in the set — so **36 Bam tiles** total.

> 🀐 The **1 Bam** is special — it's usually drawn as a **bird** instead of a bamboo stalk. Don't let it fool you!

[Visual: large display of 1 Bam through 9 Bam, with the 1 Bam highlighted]

[ Got it → ]

---

## Screen 4 of 9 — Cracks 🈶

# Cracks (Characters)

**Cracks** show a **Chinese character on top** and a **red number underneath**.

(Their full name is "Characters" — but everyone at the table calls them "Cracks.")

Same as Bams: numbered **1 through 9**, with **4 of each** = **36 Crack tiles**.

[Visual: 1 Crack through 9 Crack]

> 💡 The character on top is the *number* in Chinese. You don't need to read it — just match the red digit underneath.

[ Got it → ]

---

## Screen 5 of 9 — Dots 🔵

# Dots (Circles)

**Dots** are exactly what they sound like: circles arranged in patterns.

A 1 Dot has one circle, a 2 Dot has two, all the way up to 9 Dot.

Numbered **1 through 9**, **4 of each** = **36 Dot tiles**.

[Visual: 1 Dot through 9 Dot]

✅ **That's all 3 number suits!** Bams, Cracks, Dots — 9 numbers each, 4 of each = **108 tiles total**.

[ Quick practice → ]

## Practice Drill 1 — Suit Sorter (after Screen 5)

### Quick check: which suit?

Show one tile at a time. User taps **Bam / Crack / Dot**.

- 5 random tiles
- Instant feedback
- Must get 4/5 to continue (or "Try again" with a new set)

[After completion: "Nice — you've got the number suits down. Now for the special tiles."]

## Screen 6 of 9 — Honor tiles: Winds 🌬️

# Honor tiles, Part 1: Winds

**Honor tiles** are special — they don't have numbers.

There are **4 winds**: **East, South, West, North** (the order they sit at the table).

There are **4 of each wind** in the set = **16 Wind tiles**.

[Visual: E, S, W, N tiles displayed large]

> 💡 Memory trick: at the table, players are seated **counter-clockwise** in wind order — East goes first.

[ Next → ]

---

## Screen 7 of 9 — Honor tiles: Dragons 🐉

# Honor tiles, Part 2: Dragons

There are **3 dragons**:
- 🟥 **Red Dragon**
- 🟩 **Green Dragon**
- ⬜ **White Dragon** (also called "Soap" because it looks like a blank bar of soap!)

**4 of each** = **12 Dragon tiles**.

[Visual: Red, Green, White dragon tiles]

✅ **Honor tile total:** 16 winds + 12 dragons = **28 honor tiles**.

[ Got it → ]

---

## Screen 8 of 9 — Flowers 🌸

# Flowers

**Flowers** (sometimes called "Flowers and Seasons") are decorative tiles.

There are **8 unique flower designs**, and **only 1 of each** = **8 Flower tiles** total.

[Visual: 8 flower tiles in a row]

> 💡 Flowers are useful in certain hands. When you draw one, you usually put it on display next to your rack — but more on that in a later lesson.

[ Next → ]

---

## Screen 9 of 9 — Jokers ⭐

# Jokers — the wild card

**Jokers** are American Mahjong's secret weapon.

There are **8 Jokers** in the set, and they can stand in for **any tile in a group of 3 or more identical tiles**.

> ⚠️ **Big rule:** Jokers **cannot** be used in pairs. We'll explain why in Module 4. For now, just remember: **jokers in pungs and kongs ✅, jokers in pairs ❌.**

[Visual: a single Joker tile, slightly glowing]

✅ **Total tile count:**
- 108 number suits (Bams + Cracks + Dots)
- 28 honors (Winds + Dragons)
- 8 flowers
- 8 jokers
- = **152 tiles** ✓

[ Time to practice! → ]

## Practice Drill 2 — Speed Tap (after Screen 9)

### Tap all the dragons!

Grid of 12 random tiles. User taps every dragon (red, green, or white) as fast as they can.

- 30 second timer
- Score = correct taps − wrong taps
- 3 rounds, each with a different category:
  1. Tap all the **dragons**
  2. Tap all the **3s** (any suit)
  3. Tap all the **honor tiles** (winds + dragons)

[After: "🔥 You're getting fast! One more thing before we move on."]

## Notes for the app implementation

- **Tile rendering:** placeholder Unicode mahjong tiles (🀐🀑🀒…) work for v0; commissioned SVGs in Phase 4
- **State:** track per-screen progress in localStorage (`mahj.module1.screen`, `mahj.module1.completed`)
- **Animations:** subtle fade-in on each new tile reveal; haptic tap feedback on mobile
- **Accessibility:** every tile needs an `aria-label` ("3 of Bamboo," "Red Dragon," etc.) and color must not be the only signal (Red/Green/White dragons need text labels too)
- **Tone:** warm, encouraging, never condescending. "You're getting fast!" not "Correct!"
- **Disclaimer footer:** present, small, always visible

- Original has no practice → 2 interactive drills + 5-question check
- Original uses "Characters" formally → introduces both names ("Cracks (Characters)") since the table-name is what beginners will hear

- Joker pair rule introduced gently with promise of fuller explanation in Module 4 (no vocab dump)
